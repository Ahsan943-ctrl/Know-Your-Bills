Know Your Bills

Definitions

Voltage: Voltage is like the pressure that pushes electricity through wires. It's measured in volts (V).
Current: Current is the flow of electric charge through a wire. It's measured in amperes (A), or amps.
Power: Power is the rate at which electricity is used or produced. It's measured in watts (W). It tells you how much energy is being used per second.

Electricity Consumption Information

1 Ton AC: Consumes about 1.0 to 1.5 kWh per hour of operation. Over a month, this can be quite significant if used frequently.

1.5 Ton AC: Consumes about 1.5 to 2.0 kWh per hour. This higher capacity unit is suitable for larger rooms but consumes more electricity.

Refrigerator: Typically uses between 100 to 200 kWh per month, depending on size and efficiency. Modern refrigerators are designed to be more energy-efficient.

Electric Iron: Consumes around 1.0 to 2.0 kWh per hour of use. The actual usage depends on how long and often you use it.
